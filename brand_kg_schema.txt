==============================================================================
BRAND KNOWLEDGE GRAPH — INITIAL 2-LAYER SCHEMA
==============================================================================

ROOT NODE
---------
Brand

CHILD NODES (Layer 1) — 7 total
-------------------------------
1. Product
2. People
3. Business
4. Engineering
5. Marketing
6. Audience
7. Partnerships


==============================================================================
WHAT EACH NODE ABSORBS (so future ingestion has a clear "home")
==============================================================================

Brand          name, founding date, mission, vision, tagline, brand values,
               brand DNA, headquarters

Product        products, services, SKUs, features, pricing, availability,
               product categories, releases, roadmap items, packaging

People         founders, leadership, employees, advisors, investors-as-people,
               spokespeople, ambassadors, key hires, board members

               INTERNAL HIERARCHY — People is organized as a top-down ROLE TREE
               within each affiliation (each company/org gets its own subtree).
               Every Person carries a `seniority_tier` attribute (one of the
               9 tier strings below), and a [:REPORTS_TO] edge points from a
               person UP to a person at a higher tier in the same affiliation.

               TIER ORDER (highest authority first):
                 1. FOUNDER   — Founder, Co-Founder, Founding Partner
                 2. C_SUITE   — CEO, CFO, CTO, CMO, COO, CIO, President, Chairman
                 3. SVP       — SVP, EVP, Executive Vice President
                 4. VP        — VP, Vice President, Chief of Staff
                 5. DIRECTOR  — Director, Head of <function>, Group Director
                 6. MANAGER   — Manager, Senior Manager, Lead, Principal
                 7. IC        — Engineer, Analyst, Designer, Associate
                 8. ADVISOR   — Advisor, Board Member, Independent Director
                 9. OTHER     — does not fit any of the above

               Hierarchical edges:
                 (Person {tier=N}) -[:REPORTS_TO]-> (Person {tier<N, same affiliation})
                 (Person)          -[:AT_TIER]->   (RoleTier {tier='C_SUITE'})

               The build_people_hierarchy.py post-ingest job (in
               graphiti/self-hosted/) populates these edges. Reporting lines
               only span people in the SAME affiliation; an advisor at
               Company A does NOT report to a CEO at Company B.

Business       legal entity, ownership, revenue model, financials, funding
               rounds, operations, office locations, compliance/legal,
               M&A history, internal business units

Engineering    tech stack, infrastructure, codebases, repos, APIs, services,
               systems architecture, databases, platforms, tools used, patents,
               R&D projects, technical roadmap, security posture

Marketing      campaigns, ads, social posts, press releases, blog/content,
               brand voice, visual identity (logo, colors, typography),
               channels (website, IG, TikTok, X, YouTube), retail presence,
               sponsorships, events

Audience       customer segments, personas, demographics, reviews, testimonials,
               sentiment, competitors, market positioning, industry context,
               press coverage, mentions

Partnerships   partner organizations (suppliers, distributors, resellers,
               tech/integration partners, co-brands, sponsors, agencies,
               affiliate networks), strategic alliances, joint ventures,
               sponsorship deals, licensing deals, M&A counter-parties,
               contract terms / partnership dates


==============================================================================
LAYER-1 RELATIONS (Brand -> child)
==============================================================================

(Brand) -[:OFFERS]->            (Product)
(Brand) -[:HAS_PERSON]->        (People)
(Brand) -[:OPERATES_AS]->       (Business)
(Brand) -[:BUILT_WITH]->        (Engineering)
(Brand) -[:COMMUNICATES_VIA]->  (Marketing)
(Brand) -[:SERVES]->            (Audience)
(Brand) -[:HAS_PARTNERSHIP]->   (Partnerships)


==============================================================================
CROSS-CHILD RELATIONS (Layer-1 <-> Layer-1)
These are what make the graph multi-hop reasoning friendly.
Every node still reaches Brand within 2 hops.
==============================================================================

-- People ↔ others --
(People)      -[:FOUNDED]->         (Brand)
(People)      -[:LEADS]->           (Business)
(People)      -[:CREATED]->         (Product)
(People)      -[:WORKS_FOR]->       (Business)
(People)      -[:WORKS_ON]->        (Engineering)
(People)      -[:ENDORSES]->        (Brand)
(People)      -[:APPEARS_IN]->      (Marketing)
(People)      -[:REPORTS_TO]->      (People)         // hierarchy edge, within same affiliation
(People)      -[:AT_TIER]->         (RoleTier)       // links to one of the 9 seniority tier nodes

-- Engineering ↔ others --
(Engineering) -[:POWERS]->          (Product)
(Engineering) -[:INTEGRATES_WITH]-> (Engineering)
(Engineering) -[:OWNED_BY]->        (Business)
(Engineering) -[:USES]->            (Product)        // 3rd-party products
(Engineering) -[:BUILT_BY]->        (People)

-- Marketing ↔ others --
(Marketing)   -[:PROMOTES]->        (Product)
(Marketing)   -[:TARGETED_AT]->     (Audience)
(Marketing)   -[:FEATURES]->        (People)
(Marketing)   -[:RUN_BY]->          (Business)

-- Product ↔ others --
(Product)     -[:SOLD_THROUGH]->    (Business)
(Product)     -[:TARGETED_AT]->     (Audience)
(Product)     -[:DEPENDS_ON]->      (Engineering)
(Product)     -[:PROMOTED_BY]->     (Marketing)

-- Audience ↔ others --
(Audience)    -[:REVIEWS]->         (Product)
(Audience)    -[:MENTIONS]->        (Brand)
(Audience)    -[:COMPARED_TO]->     (Brand)         // competitor mentions
(Audience)    -[:RESPONDS_TO]->     (Marketing)

-- Business ↔ others --
(Business)    -[:HAS_AGREEMENT_WITH]-> (Partnerships)  // formal contracts
(Business)    -[:DISTRIBUTES]->     (Product)
(Business)    -[:EMPLOYS]->         (People)
(Business)    -[:INVESTS_IN]->      (Engineering)

-- Partnerships ↔ others --
(Partnerships) -[:SUPPLIES]->        (Product)        // supplier relationship
(Partnerships) -[:DISTRIBUTES]->     (Product)        // distribution/reseller
(Partnerships) -[:CO_BRANDS_WITH]->  (Brand)          // co-branded products/campaigns
(Partnerships) -[:SPONSORS]->        (Marketing)      // sponsorship deals
(Partnerships) -[:INTEGRATES_WITH]-> (Engineering)    // tech/API partners
(Partnerships) -[:REPRESENTED_BY]->  (People)         // partner-side liaison
(Partnerships) -[:OPERATES_IN]->     (Audience)       // partner reaches a segment
(Partnerships) -[:PARTNERS_WITH]->   (Partnerships)   // partner-of-partner chains

    Brand -HAS_PARTNERSHIP-> Partnerships -INTEGRATES_WITH-> Engineering -POWERS-> Product


==============================================================================
INGESTION HINTS (how new facts attach)
==============================================================================

When extracting a new fact about a brand, ask: which of the 6 buckets does
the subject of the fact belong to? Attach it as a child of that node (Layer 2+).

Examples:
  "Acme launched Widget Pro in March"      -> new Product subnode
  "Jane Doe is CTO"                        -> new People subnode + WORKS_FOR Business
  "Series B raised $40M from Sequoia"      -> new Business subnode (funding round)
  "Backend runs on Postgres + Kafka"       -> new Engineering subnodes
  "TikTok campaign #AcmeMoment"            -> new Marketing subnode -TARGETED_AT-> Audience
  "Gen Z users on Reddit say..."           -> new Audience subnode -MENTIONS-> Brand
  "Acme partners with Stripe for payments" -> new Partnerships subnode
                                              -INTEGRATES_WITH-> Engineering
  "Nike x Off-White collaboration"         -> new Partnerships subnode
                                              -CO_BRANDS_WITH-> Brand
  "Acme sponsors F1 team Williams"         -> new Partnerships subnode
                                              -SPONSORS-> Marketing

Every leaf node, however deep, traces back to Brand via these typed edges.
